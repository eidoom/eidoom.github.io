Simulating continuous symmetry breaking with cold atoms in optical cavities

My final-year undergraduate research project, supervised by Jonathan Keeling and Kyle Ballantine, involved modelling non-equilibrium quantum systems to explore the continuous symmetries they can exhibit. Before starting original work, I conducted a literature review, summarised in my report (source). I then developed a Python simulation of the two-mode Dicke model to study its quantum phases. I wrote up the project in my dissertation (source). I also presented a poster (source) and defended the work in my viva with a presentation (source).

During the following summer and on into my MASt at Cambridge, I continued work on the project. We extended the model, leading to a publication (arXiv). The rich behaviour of the system is evident in my notes (source). I also gave summary presentations of the work (source) during my PhD interviews.
